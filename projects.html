Selected Assignments

A curated selection of our completed research, evaluation, survey, and programme management assignments across Pakistan.

Evaluation

Endline Evaluation: Scale-Up of Innovative Approach to Family Planning in LHW Rural Uncovered Areas, 2025–26

End-Line Survey and Evaluation commissioned by the Punjab Population Innovation Fund (PPIF) to evaluate the effectiveness of the NOOR Call programme across ten districts of Punjab, Pakistan. Data collection completed July–August 2025, achieving a total household sample of 9,654 Married Women of Reproductive Age — a very positive response rate against the planned sample across all operational units.

2025–26 PPIF
Survey & Research

Baseline Study on Scale-Up of Male Engagement in Family Planning Program, 2025

Baseline Study for the integration of male health workers and service providers in the family planning ecosystem in 3 districts (4 units) of Punjab. Provided PPIF with genuine insights into key indicators closely related to project interventions and reliable data to inform decision making.

2025 PPIF
Monitoring

Third Party Monitoring — Punjab Family Planning Programme, 2025

Third Party Monitoring conducted in 4 Units across 3 districts of Punjab: Hafizabad, Narowal, and Mandi Bahauddin.

2025 PPIF
Monitoring

Third Party Monitoring — Punjab Family Planning Programme, 2022

Third Party Monitoring across Lahore, Rawalpindi, Sialkot, Attock, Khushab, Rajanpur, and Jhang.

2022 PPIF
Survey & Research

Baseline Study on Knowledge, Attitudes and Practices (KAP) — Family Planning, 2022

KAP Baseline studies in Sialkot, Lahore, Rawalpindi, Faisalabad, Sargodha, and Toba Tek Singh to inform family planning programme design and implementation.

2022 PPIF
Monitoring

Third Party Monitoring — Punjab Family Planning Programme, 2021–22

Third Party Monitoring across Punjab province for PPIF's family planning programme interventions.

2021–22 PPIF
Evaluation

End-of-Project Assessment: Scaling Zinc & Low Osmolality ORS for Childhood Diarrhea

Assessment conducted across 15 districts in Punjab and Sindh (6 Sindh, 9 Punjab) to assess outcomes of Nutrition International's scaled-up interventions for treating childhood diarrhea with zinc and ORS, following a successful pilot in district Gujrat.

2018–19 Nutrition International
Survey & Research

Equity Baseline Assessment of Islamabad City — URBAN HEART Methodology for Healthy City Programme

Data collected from five sectors of Islamabad to assess health-seeking behaviours, knowledge, and practices of communities in settled and slum areas for WHO's Healthy City Programme launch.

2018–19 WHO
Evaluation

Evaluation of LHW Involvement in Routine EPI After EPI Training — 72 Districts Across Pakistan

A total of 38,900 LHWs were trained across 72 districts in AJK, Balochistan, KPK, Punjab, and Sindh. Mixed methods: 3,312 LHW survey sample, 303 IDIs with district and provincial managers, and 36 FGDs with LHWs to assess practical involvement in routine immunisation.

2016–17 WHO
Baseline Survey

Baseline Data Collection in 6 Districts of Gilgit-Baltistan — AQCESS Project

Aga Khan Foundation's MNCH project aimed at improving outcomes for women of reproductive age and children under five. Three data collection teams collected data from 1,540 respondents in over 1,150 households across 25 days using CAPI on tablets.

Aug–Sep 2016 Aga Khan Foundation
Training

Development of Training Modules and Training of Afghan Students

Developed three training modules (Entrepreneurship & Business Plan; Marketing; Job Searching & Resume Writing) each with Trainer's Manual, Trainee's Manual, and Presentation. Trained 120 Afghan students in four batches across three sessions per batch.

Jul–Aug 2016 Islamabad
Training

Training of CARE International's SAFPAC Project Staff on Monitoring & Evaluation

Specifically designed M&E training for SAFPAC staff incorporating project monitoring tools and methodology. Covered M&E concepts, methodologies, tools, and interventions for management, field monitoring staff, and implementing partners.

Dec 2015–Jan 2016 CARE International
Impact Assessment

Impact Assessment of SAFPAC Project Interventions on Women's Well-being

SAFPAC project addressed unmet needs for family planning and post-abortion care in 29 health facilities in Muzaffargarh and Multan. Assessment determined service coverage, awareness, changes in gender attitudes, shifts in social norms, and women's empowerment outcomes using mixed methods including community surveys.

Dec 2015–Jan 2016 CARE International
Survey & Research

Assessment of Social Protection Schemes and Nutritional Status — WINS Programme, Sindh

EU-funded WINS project (Women & Children Improved Nutrition in Sindh). Assessment covered social protection initiatives (BISP), institutional coordination, linkage with WINS, and recommendations based on mixed method evidence including quantitative and qualitative components in Shikarpur, Sindh.

Sep–Nov 2015 MDM France / EU
Survey & Research

Assessment of Perceptions, Knowledge and Utilisation of Family Planning Methods — 102 Districts

Largest national family planning KAP assessment covering 239 tehsils in 102 districts across Pakistan (LAD, DFID, and KfW funded components). Total sample of 8,921 households drawn; data collected from 9,249 households nationally using mixed methods.

Apr 2014–Feb 2015 GreenStar Pakistan
Baseline Study

Baseline Study for IKEA-Funded Project — Cotton Growing Districts, Sindh & Punjab

Comprehensive baseline using household surveys (1,268 HH), FGDs, IDIs, school and health facility profiling, and laboratory investigations for iodine levels and worm infestation in 350 communities across 15 union councils in Nawabshah and Lodhran.

Jul–Nov 2014 IKEA / UNICEF
Survey & Research

Baseline KAP Study on WASH in Four Districts of Punjab

Plan International Pakistan's 4-year open defecation elimination project targeting 280,000 population. KAP survey across Layyah, Rahim Yar Khan, Vehari, and Chakwal: 928 household surveys, 16 FGDs, and 12 IDIs with officials from PHED, Health and Education departments.

Nov 2013–Jan 2014 Plan International
Formative Research

Formative Research on WASH Using FOAM Framework — Four Districts of Punjab

Research used the FOAM (Focus, Opportunity, Ability, Motivation) Framework to identify determinants of WASH-related behaviours and gaps in BCC materials. Mixed methods: population survey, FGDs with community members, and document review.

Dec 2013–Feb 2014 Plan International
Evaluation

Evaluation of Community-Based Health Services for Afghan Refugees — Balochistan

American Refugee Committee provides services for 100,000+ population in Quetta and Pishin. Evaluation covered primary healthcare, reproductive health, GBV services, and livelihood programmes. Mixed methods: 540 household community survey and 25 IDIs with programme beneficiaries.

Dec 2013 American Refugee Committee
Survey

Determining the Extent of Individual Philanthropy in Sindh Province — 26 Districts

Pakistan Center for Philanthropy survey to gauge individual philanthropy practices across Sindh. Cross-sectional survey of 3,000 households in 26 districts with representation from rural and urban areas — covering attitudes, motivations, and practices around charitable giving.

Jun–Sep 2013 Pakistan Center for Philanthropy
Survey & Research

KAP Assessment for Family Planning and Post-Abortion Care Programme — Sindh & KPK

Save the Children programme to increase availability of modern contraceptive and PAC services in 13 health facilities. Study determined knowledge, attitudes, and practices of women and men of reproductive age and service providers. Mixed methods including FGDs, IDIs, and household surveys in Shikarpur (Sindh) and Haripur (KPK).
